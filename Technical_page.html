Introduction

This is a quick overview of the correct and safe methods of testing fixed wire electrical installations. Following the correct proceedures will ensure that you remain safe during the process and that risk of damage to the installation is minimised.

Familiarity with electrics

Before you undertake the testing of an installation, it is important that you are familiar with how a circuit is installed and the devices connected. In the UK, we have common features that appear in an installation. Some of these include the use of: ring final circuits, radial circuits, wiring in twin and earth cable.

A standard domestic installation would have some, if not all of the following:
32A cooker or hob circuits wired in 6 mm to 10 mm
32A immersion circuit wired in 2.5 mm
32A ring final circuits wired in 2.5 mm or 4 mm
20A radial power circuits wired in 2.5 mm
6A radial lighting circuits wired in 1.5 mm or 1 mm

It's also important to understand how the switch gear works, how it is selected and how it is populated in the distribution board. There are electrical wiring regulation that determine selection and erection of devices. The regs should be consulted if and when there is uncertainty as to the correct selection of such equipment.

There are other factors involved with testing and that includes familiarity with how the wiring may have been installed. Knowing how to identfy if rinds or radials are used, which way from the dist board might have been chosed to wire and splits of sockets or points scattered within the building.

Choice of equipment

The choice of the correct electrical testing equipment is vital in ensuring both safety and recieving the correct readings to determine the condition of the installation. A basic 18th edition multi function tester will perform all the test required, some just do it easier.

Testers as a basic requirement must comply with GS38 regulation, some of which the points to conform to are:

  • Probe tips must be no longer than 4 mm or be retractable
  • Probes must be fused inline
  • Probes and leads must be identifiable by different colours
  • Must have finger barriers

Dead and live testing

Dead testing is as it says, performed with the whole installation de-energised. A proving unit should be used to confirm. There are a number of tests to be performed and they are:

  • Continuity of earth connectors
  • R1 and R2
  • Insulation resistance
  • Separation of circuits
  • Polarity
